Abstract

Golf performance depends on precise coordination between the player and the club. Most vision systems focus on body pose, missing the club motion needed to analyze swing speed, trajectory, and carry-at-impact. We address this with CADDIE, a compact markerless two-stage framework tailored to thin elongated geometry, motion blur, and self-occlusion in golf club pose estimation.

Key contributions:

  • GolfClub dataset — 72K frames, 7 subjects, 6 locations, 3 club types (driver / wood / iron) with a universal five-keypoint schema.
  • CADDIE architecture — only 14.3M params (9.5M detection + 4.8M pose) running at 69 FPS, outperforming HRNet (28.6M) and ViTPose (90M).
  • Adaptive temporal smoothing — scales 69 → 238 FPS with <1% accuracy loss.

CADDIE achieves 88.52% PCK@5px, a leap over HRNet (72.90%) and ViTPose (42.17%).

Challenges in Golf Club Pose

Motion Blur

Fast swing speeds combined with low shutter speeds or dim lighting smear the club into ghost-like trails across frames.

Self-Occlusion

The shaft is repeatedly hidden by the player's hands and body.

Limited Viewpoint

Most prior work tracks the club from a single front view, missing the head-face angle essential for the club face at impact.

Single Club Type

Existing studies evaluate on one club (typically a driver or an iron).

Method

Two-stage top-down pipeline:

  • Stage 1 — Detection. ConvNeXtV2-Atto [1] + FPN + RetinaNet [2] head (9.5M params, 8.4 ms).
  • Stage 2 — Pose. ConvNeXtV2-Atto [1] + FPN with parallel heatmap + offset heads on a 360×360 crop (4.8M params, 4.0 ms). Sub-pixel keypoint extraction via argmax + offset refinement.
  • Losses. Focal + EIoU bounding-box regression (Stage 1); Gaussian heatmap MSE + L1 offset (Stage 2).

Adaptive Temporal Smoothing

Stage 1 dominates the latency budget. We exploit the temporal smoothness of bounding boxes during a swing by running detection every N-th frame and interpolating in between:

  • Multi-level interpolation — Linear → Quadratic refinement → Cubic Hermite fallback when oscillation is detected.
  • Stationarity detector — locks frames during the address phase to prevent drift.
  • Per-frame Kalman + EMA — smooths box centers (Kalman) and dimensions (EMA) separately.

Conclusions

  • Real-time, markerless golf club pose estimation with a compact 14.3M two-stage design.
  • Universal 5-keypoint schema generalizes across drivers, woods, and irons.
  • Outperforms HRNet, ViTPose, and YOLO on every keypoint while running 25× faster than HRNet.
  • Adaptive temporal smoothing scales deployment from 69 → 238 FPS for live coaching.
  • Future: 3D extension via a multi-camera system built on our 2D pose method.
